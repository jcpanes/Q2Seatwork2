CAT BREEDS

Cat Breed Description
American Bobtail The American Bobtail is an athletic breed with many dog-like traits and has the appearance of a bob-tailed wildcat.
Egyptian Mau The Egyptian Mau is very devoted to its human family and vocally expresses its joy and affection by meowing in a soothing tone.
Norwegian Forest A gentle giant, the Norwegian Forest Cat. They enjoy spending time with their family but are reserved around others.
Ragdoll Ragdolls are playful, intelligent, and loving. They greet their people, follow them around, sit on their laps, and cuddle up in bed to show their love for them.
Pixiebob The Pixiebob is a domestic cat with the affectionate personality of a wild Coastal Red Bobcat in the Pacific Northwest. It is a strong, muscular cat.
Somali With their full bushy tail and britches, large ears, and dramatically ticked coat, the Somali is strikingly beautiful. They have the appearance of a small wild fox.
Toyger The Toyger is smart and easy to train since it walks on a leash and plays fetch. In general, the Toyger is healthy and robust.
Turkish Angora The Turkish Angora is considered a national treasure in her home country of Turkey, despite the fact that it was the first longhair cat breed in Europe.
Turkish Van The Turkish Van's long, plumed tail, colored markings on her head, and chalk-white body make it stand out.
LaPerm The LaPerm is recognizable by its curly, rippled coat, and people-oriented personality. At times, they are energetic and curious, but they are also calm and friendly.
